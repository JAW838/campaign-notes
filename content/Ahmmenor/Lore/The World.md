The World is covered by three main continents: the eastern continent of [[Feyrndel]], the central continent of [[Ahmmenor]], and the western continent of [[Drisalygg]] (not pictured). They have weathered the storm of time, and are inhabited by some of the most impressive and resilient beings in all the planes.
# Timeline
Due to the influence of the [[Gulfen#Elven Kings of Gulfen|Elven Kings of Gulfen]], the various Ages are marked by their rule. Additionally, [[The Nimyren Empire]] uses its own year system: BNU (Before Nimyren Unification) and NU (Nimyren Unification).
## The Dawn War
---
Before history began, creation trembled at the destruction wrought by [[The Dawn War]], a devastating conflict between the powers that be. Little but legends and ancient whispers have survived to tell the tale of this beyond ancient conflict, and they make no mention of its length or cause.
However, the end of the war marks the beginning of the First Winter.
### The First Winter
A long and strenuous winter which few survived. The cause and length of the Winter is unknown. Its end brought about The First Spring and marks the beginning of the first Age, the Age of Myth.
## The Age of Myth (0-10,000)
---
Beginning with The First Spring and the emergence of the Erlking, stretching to the War of Divine Egress, the Age of Myth was a time of peace and prosperity.
### The First Spring
[[The World#The First Winter|The First Winter]], the harshest and most brutal winter, was followed by the First Spring, the most peaceful and prosperous spring. Survivors of the Winter began to rebuild what they had lost, and were guided by the gods themselves as they walked among the people and guided civilizations towards a brighter future. This was a time of celebration, thanksgiving, and comradery.
### The Erlking
During this time, the first King of the Elves, commonly referred to as the [[Erlking]], sailed to [[Ahmmenor]] from lands unknown, aiding in the establishment of [[Gulfen]] as one of the world's earliest societies and establishing his [[Gulfen#The Alabaster Throne|Alabaster Throne]] as an important authority within the world at large.
His death during [[The World#The War of Divine Egress|The War of Divine Egress]] marks the end of the Age of Myth and the beginning of not only the Age of Progress but the reign of his son [[Neurodorin]].
## The Age of Progress (10,000-15,000)
---
[[Neurodorin|King Neurodorin]] came to power during [[The World#The War of Divine Egress|The War of Divine Egress]], the greatest conflict since [[The Dawn War]]. He successfully navigated the disaster and went on to be instrumental in the advancement of civilisation until his death.
### The War of Divine Egress
Grudges between greater powers held since [[The Dawn War]] finally reached their breaking point, boiling over and resulting in a war that reshaped the very continents themselves. One of the first killed in this conflict was the [[Erlking]] himself, and it is believed whichever god was responsible for his death cursed his throne so that the rein of each following king was shorter than the last.
### Reign of King Neurodorin
After leading his forces through the war, the Elven King spent the remainder of his life aiding development of arcane magics so civilisation could thrive in the absence of direct divine intervention. He directed his high mages to repair what damages they could, including sealing planar rifts and binding or slaying many Great Beasts of Myth. These acts created a safer world for future generations.
What ultimately caused his untimely death is lost to history, although foul play could never be entirely ruled out.
## The Age of Hubris (15,000-17,500)
---
[[Belodrin|Belodrin the Baned]], the heir to [[Gulfen#The Alabaster Throne|The Alabaster Throne]], oversaw the establishment of numerous metropolises across his land and reckless magical experimentation in an attempt to cure his illness.
### Reign of King Belodrin the Baned
[[Belodrin]] was obsessed by the supposed curse placed on the throne which he had inherited, and his fear of it motivated his every action. His paranoia led him to drive his people to "Rië Empatyellë", or "Only Advance", a mantra that would create vast sprawling cities filled with arcane wonders in his name. The effects of these wonders, both positive and negative, continue to shape the world today. 
During the latter years of his reign, he pushed extensively for reckless experimentation as he began to succumb to an unknown and seemingly incurable illness. While it would ultimately take his life, he would live to see a new kind of cataclysm: [[The Shattering]].
### The Shattering
Nobody one knows what tipped the scales on that fateful day. Perhaps it was punishment from the gods, a foolish mage attempted a magic too outlandishly vast for the weave to strain, or the pressure of all the magics of the world had simply become too much. Whatever the reason, on one fateful day the world broke, and magic itself lay shattered across the world in a cataclysm hither to undreamt of.
Much of the world suffered as their magics had either been sundered, erased, or in the worst cases tainted by an apparent rot within the weave of magic itself. Others sealed themselves away with what little stable magic remained, including the now feeble [[Belodrin|King Belodrin]] and his subjects, although the King would succumb to illness at some point during this event.
Specific dates are hard to define during [[The Shattering]] as things as intrinsic as time itself were thrown off their rhythm, however our best guess would have no less than 1000 years from its beginning to the emergence of [[Valcore]] once again at the end of the Inquisitions last crusade in [[Ahmmenor]].
## Rein of the Fourth Elf King (17,500-Present)
---
The [[Levitas|Fourth Elf King Levitas]] gained control of [[Gulfen]] after [[The Shattering]]. He brought his Elves out of hiding when the chaos subsided and [[The Inquisition]] had fallen apart, although peace with the new Human occupants was short-lived, and the tension resulted in [[The Nimyren War]]. This war was brought to a close when [[Emperor Ballas]] united the warring countries into what is now [[The Nimyren Empire]].
### The Inquisition (528 BNU-415 BNU)
[[The Shattering]] left magic crippled for a long time. When it began to regain its former strength, many were concerned that magic users would push the weave too far, causing a second disaster, and they formed [[The Inquisition]] to stop this. They surged across [[Ahmmenor]], destroying any magical knowledge they found along the way. In this, they were successful, and little magical knowledge from before this time remains.
During this time, [[Arkantos]] was founded as a refuge for those persecuted by the crusade.
### Valcore and the Others Emerge (415 BNU)
The Kingdom of Elves and Kingdom of Dwarves emerged during this time, having sequestered themselves away to protect against the destruction of [[The Shattering]]. They were shocked to find their lands had been inhabited by Humans, and tensions ran high as the Kingdoms searched for a place to call home.
### The Nimyren War (400 BNU-0 NU)
Tensions reached their breaking point, and [[The Nimyren War]] broke out between the Humans and the Kingdoms of Dwarves and Elves. This long and bloody conflict was brought to an end when a then-unknown figure emerged, uniting the warring factions and ending the fighting. He would come to be known as [[Emperor Ballas]].
