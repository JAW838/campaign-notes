# Notes
- [[Roving Seven]] arrives in a nearby town and stay at the home of [[Bartel B.]] and his father. They stayed there for 5 days while [[Diabel Songlark]] recuperated. During this time they participated in the local gig economy, went shopping, and fought crime as vigilantes.
- [[Sylas Blackstone|Sylas]] then arrived with a caravan. [[Leon Redbrand|Leon]] shared the recovered research with him, and the visitor recommended reading the following files: Botanical Research, Weapon Tech, and Quintessense Research.
- [[Sylas Blackstone|Sylas]] reveals he has been promoted and has more power and resources. Apparently [[Emperor Ballas]] is happy with his performance.
- [[Sylas Blackstone|Sylas]] wants to hire the [[Roving Seven]] to finish off the [[Tech Hunters]], who are hiding near an ancient city of metal half-buried in [[The Scarlet Sands]], a deduction made with [[Leon Redbrand|Leon]]'s recent infiltration of the base and [[Sylas Blackstone|Sylas]]'s knowledge of the area. However, the contract must be negotiated and signed in [[The Nimyren Empire#Narmor|Narmor]], the capitol city of [[The Nimyren Empire]]. To expedite their arrival, he lends them an airship which will take them there.
- Because it is a short detour, [[Leon Redbrand|Leon]]'s proposal to visit the [[Blood Hunters]] for personal reasons on the way is accepted.
- In working with the government, blanket pardons for past crimes are on the table. [[Diabel Songlark]] receives a letter of recommendation from him for a pardon to reenter his home territory. [[Ondo Edafoss]] was pardoned for several crimes including crushings, lying about the rock from the docks, and [[Ondo Edafoss#Fish-Related Crimes|"Fish-Related Crimes"]]. These pardons are already in place or are expected to be accepted, while the rest should be passed once the deal is signed in [[The Nimyren Empire#Narmor|Narmor]].
- [[Sylas Blackstone|Sylas]] is intrigued with the possibility of [[Richard]] being an imperial soldier.
